Elizabeth Blackwell

(1821-1910)

Elizabeth Blackwell was the first woman to earn a medical degree and become a doctor. She made a huge step for women around the world, and opened the door of medicine for women.

Here is a timeline of her life:

  • 1821 - Elizabeth Blackwell was born in England
  • 1832 - her family moved to New York when she was 11 years old
  • 1839 - Elizabeth became a teacher so that she could earn money. She wanted to go to medical school.
  • 1845 - When Elizabeth tried to enter medical school, no one would take her because she was a woman. She moved to North Carolina where she studied under a local doctor.
  • 1847 - Geneva College in New York finally accepted Elizabeth as a practical joke. She moved to New York right away.
  • 1848 - Elizabeth was called a pioneer for women. This was because of her determination to be a doctor.
  • 1849 - Elizabeth became the first woman to graduate from medical school. However, no hospital would hire her, so she moved to Paris to practice medicine there.
  • 1857 - Elizabeth returned to New York but still no one would hire her. She decided to open her own practice for women and children.
  • 1861 - During the Civil War, Elizabeth trained many women to work as nurses for the wounded soldiers. President Lincoln praised the work of the women during the war.
  • 1868 - Elizabeth earned a good reputation during the war, and she was finally accepted among the people. She opened her own college to train women in the medical field.
  • 1869 - Elizabeth traveled with Florence Nightingale to England, where she opened up another college for women. She was the first woman on the medical register in England.
  • 1871 - Elizabeth wrote a book called The Religion of Health. She talked about cleanliness and being healthy.
  • 1875 - Elizabeth worked hard and became a professor of gynecology at the London School of Medicine.
  • 1907 - Elizabeth retired from teaching and practicing medicine when she had an accident that she never fully recovered.
  • 1910 - died at the age of 89 in England
